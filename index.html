USA Train Accidents 2012 - 2022


Welcome! The purpose of this tool is:
(1) to understand the causes of train accidents over the last ten years and help identify potential safety hazards
(2) to raise public awareness to be more vigilant and cautious around train and railroads
(3) to identify areas where regulations and laws need to be updated or changed to ensure compliance and prevent future accidents

Railroad line data was obtained from ESRI and the NGDA (National Geospatial Data Asset).

Map icons use Noun Project graphics from Daniele Catalanotto (derail), Tomas Knopp (fire), Prashanth Rapolu (head-on collision; broken train collision), SAM Designs (obstruction), kasign23 (other impacts), Gan Khoon Lay (highway-rail crossing), walkerstudio13 (raking), Matt Caisley (rear collision), Adrien Coquet (side-hit), and Adi Kurniawan (train station).

This site was created by UW Madison students Domonic Cwalinski and Lisa Siewert in May 2023.
